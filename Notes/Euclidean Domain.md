``` ad-Definition
title: Definition.

_Let $R$ be an integral domain $R$. We say that $R$ is a **Euclidean Domain** if there exists a function $N:R\to\N$ such that for all $a,b\in R$ with $b\neq0$, there exist $q,r\in R$ with either $r=0$ or $N\l(r\r)<N\l(b\r)$ such that $a=qb+r$._
```
