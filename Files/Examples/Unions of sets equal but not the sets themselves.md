**Example.** Consider the sets $A=\l\{\l\{0,1\r\},\l\{2\r\}\r\}$ and $B=\l\{\l\{0\r\},\l\{1,2\r\}\r\}$. Observe that $\l\{2\r\}\in A$ but $\l\{2\r\}\not\in B$, so we have $A\neq B$. However, we have
$$\begin{equation}
    \bigcup A=\bigcup B=\l\{0,1,2\r\}.\exqedin
\end{equation}$$
